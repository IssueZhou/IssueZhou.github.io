Research

Near-field Thermal Radiation for Optomechanics
Ongoing

With Raphael St-Gelais. Under the supervision of Prof. Jack Sankey.

We use near-field thermal transfer between parallel structures to study the cooling or excitation of the mechanical modes.

As an undergrad, I automated the data collection system for mode imaging based on a high vacuum fiber interferometer and Microelectromechanical Systems (MEMS) and measured the bolometric damping and antidamping. I also simulated the mode behaviors of the MEMS.

Optically Defined Localization
Ongoing

Independent research.Under the supervision of Prof. Jack Sankey.

The directional band gap is studied by constructing an asymmetric 2D phononic crystal structure in COMSOL. Then I analytically calculate and simulate the band structures from different directions of this special phononic crystal. Based on this, I further explore the mechanical modes under optical traps of different optical spring strengths. The localization modes induced by defect are also studied.

Liquid Droplet as Self-powered Temperature and Force sensor

With Kang Liu. Co-first author. Under the supervision of Prof. Jun Zhou.

We report a self-powered multimodal temperature and force sensor based on the reverse electrowetting effect and the thermogalvanic effect in a liquid droplet. The deformation of the droplet and the temperature difference across the droplet can induce an alternating pulse voltage and a direct voltage, respectively, which is easy to separate/analyze and can be utilized to sense the external force and temperature simultaneously. In addition, an integral display system that can derive information from external temperature/force concurrently is constructed.

Check out the Angew. Chem. paper [1].

Wearable Thermocells Based on Gel Electrolytes

With Peihua Yang, et al. Under the supervision of Prof. Jun Zhou.

To avoid the limitations of traditional solid-state thermoelectric materials, such as frangibility and complex fabrication processes, we fabricated two types of thermogalvanic gel electrolytes with positive and negative thermo-electrochemical Seebeck coefficients, respectively, which correspond to the n-type and p-type elements of a conventional thermoelectric generator. Based on these electrolytes, a flexible and wearable thermocell was designed with an output voltage approaching 1 V by utilizing body heat.

Check out the Angew. Chem. paper [2].

Flexible Microfluidics Nanogenerator

With Kang Liu, et al. Under the supervision of Prof. Jun Zhou.

We present a flexible and reliable microfluidics nanogenerator (MFNG), which can convert environmental mechanical energy into electricity by utilizing the streaming potential effect. A MFNG with the effective sectional area of 1 mm2 can produce a pulse voltage of ~1.5 V and current of ~1 μA. The output performance of the MFNG can be further promoted by optimizing the type of fluidic liquid, concentration of solution, surface property of microchannels as well as the size of the microchannels. Moreover, we demonstrate the application of the MFNG by powering a LCD directly with a gentle finger press and lighting a LED after charging a capacitor.

Check out the Nano Energy paper [3].

Graphene as Saturable Absorber for Mode-locking Lasers

Undergraduate research training project. Under the supervision of Prof. Guangzhi Zhu.

We performed a literature review to study the characteristics and mechanisms of graphene as a saturable absorber, as well as the Haus Master Equation which describes the passive mode-locking pulse process. The split-step Fourier transform was used to obtain the numerical solution. Based on result, the differences between graphene and semiconductor saturable absorber mirror (SESAM) as saturable absorbers were analysed.

Projects

To Sweep the Space or Not?
2016

With Hengda He and Jingbo Wang.
Honorable Mention of Mathematical Contest in Modeling of America (MCM)

Using Matlab, we developed a mathematical model for debris distribution and satellites orbits and calculated the collision probability. Considering benefit of removing debris from two parts: 1) the reduction of the collision probability of the launching satellites; 2) the reduction of the maneuvering probability of the in-orbit satellites, we used a genetic algorithm to optimize the debris removing plan for obtaining a maximum profit. Apart from financial benefit, we developed a fuzzy comprehensive evaluation model to consider other factors, like risk, cost, application, policy, and effect. Based on our models, we provided suggestions for private firms of the potential of the debris removing business.

K-SVD Dictionary Learning Sparse Coding for Image De-noising
2015

With Hengda He and Ke Chen.
2nd Prize in Mathorcup and CAA International Mathematical Contest of Modeling.

A popular method for image de-noising is to use sparse representation, like K-SVD, which is a dictionary learning algorithm. In this work, we extended the K-SVD image de-noising method, to further improve its performance. First, the image was decomposed into overlapping blocks. Second, blocks were clustered into 8 categories based on its intra-block spatial distribution, and the image was segmented into 8 compartments. Last, K-SVD was applied to each compartment for de-noising, and de-noised compartments are combined together to obtain the de-noised image. We validated our extension using PSNR on the cameraman image and compared our results with 5 other sparse coding de-noisng methods: 1) K-SVD; 2) DCT; 3) DWT; 4) SVD; 5) PCA. Our experiments showed that the best methods are in order: Our method > K-SVD > DCT > DWT > PCA > SVD.

Smart Home Automation System through Mobile Phones
2015

We are using Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) to control the devices connected to it by Bluetooth. An APP is written for Android mobile phones, through which users can control the LEDs, set the alarm clock, play music on home-made speakers, and read the temperature and humidity of the room. The whole system is developed based on a Basys 2 Spartan-3E FPGA Trainer board.

Borehole TV Camera System
2014

We build a geoexploration system comprised of a probe, a compass, a camera, 3 microcontrollers and an iPad. The depth and direction of the probe, the video taken from the camera, and the humidity data taken from the probe sensor will all be processed by the microcontrollers and then sent to iPad to present to users. The system is a product of a new start-up company, Wuhan Huake Xunce Technology Co., Ltd, aimed to ease the geotechnical engineers' lives.
