Research

Near-field thermal radiation for optomechanics between parallel structures (Ongoing)

With Raphael St-Gelais. Under the supervision of Prof. Jack Sankey.

We use near-field thermal transfer between parallel SiC nanobeams in the deep subwavelength regime to study the cooling or excitation of the mechanical modes. The distance between the beams is controlled by a high-precision micro-electromechanical system (MEMS). A fiber interferometer system is constructed to measure the mechanical modes.

Optically defined directional localization (Ongoing)

Under the supervision of Prof. Jack Sankey. Independent research.

The directional band gap is studied by constructing an asymmetric 2D phononic crystal structure in COMSOL. Then we calculate and simulate the band structures from different directions of this special phononic crystal. Based on this, we further explore the mechanical modes under optical traps of different optical spring strengths. The localization modes induced by defect are also studied.



Liquid droplet as self-powered temperature and force sensor

With Kang Liu. Co-first author, paper submitted to Angewandte Chemie. Under the supervision of Prof. Jun Zhou.

We report a self-powered multimodal temperature and force sensor based on the reverse electrowetting effect and the thermogalvanic effect in a liquid droplet. The deformation of the droplet and the temperature difference across the droplet can induce an alternating pulse voltage and a direct voltage, respectively, which is easy to separate/analyze and can be utilized to sense the external force and temperature simultaneously. In addition, an integral display system that can derive information from external temperature/force concurrently is constructed.

Wearable Thermocells Based on Gel Electrolytes for the Utilization of Body Heat

With Peihua Yang, et al. Under the supervision of Prof. Jun Zhou.
[Angew. Chem. Int. Ed. 2016, 55, 12050–12053]

To avoid the limitations of traditional solid-state thermoelectric materials, such as frangibility and complex fabrication processes, we fabricated two types of thermogalvanic gel electrolytes with positive and negative thermo-electrochemical Seebeck coefficients, respectively, which correspond to the n-type and p-type elements of a conventional thermoelectric generator. Based on these electrolytes, a flexible and wearable thermocell was designed with an output voltage approaching 1 V by utilizing body heat.



Graphene as saturable absorber for mode-locking lasers

Undergraduate research training project. Under the supervision of Prof. Guangzhi Zhu.

We perform a literature review to study the characteristics and mechanisms of graphene as a saturable absorber, as well as the Haus Master Equation which describes the passive mode-locking pulse process. The split-step Fourier transform is used to obtain the numerical solution. Based on result, the differences between graphene and semiconductor saturable absorber mirror (SESAM) as saturable absorbers are analysed.

Projects

Smart Home automation system through mobile phones

We are using Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) as controller to control the devices connected to it by Bluetooth. An APP is written for Android mobile phones, through which users can control the LEDs, set the alarm clock, play music on home-made speakers, and read the temperature and humidity of the room. The whole system is developed based on a Basys 2 Spartan-3E FPGA Trainer board.

Borehole TV camera system

We build a geoexploration system comprised of a probe, a compass, a camera, 3 microcontrollers and an iPad. The depth and direction of the probe, the video taken from the camera, and the humidity data taken from the probe sensor will all be processed by the microcontrollers and then sent to iPad to present to users. The system is a product of a new start-up company aimed to ease the geotechnical engineers' lives.

Volunteer for mental health patients

We volunteer in Wuhan Youfu Hospital to keep company with mental health patients. Because of prejudice, people with mental illness are likely to be excluded even by their own families! This makes their re-entry into the community very difficult. Our job is to chat with them, tell them about the stories happening outside the hospital high walls, and ease their loneliness and fear.
